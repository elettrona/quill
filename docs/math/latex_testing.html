Algebra Formulas

1. Quadratic Formula

Used to find the roots of a quadratic equation: $$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

2. Difference of Squares

A standard algebraic identity for factoring polynomials: $$a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b)$$

3. Binomial Theorem

Describes the algebraic expansion of powers of a binomial: $$(a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k$$

4. Logarithm Change of Base

Used to convert logarithms from one base to another: $$\log_a(x) = \frac{\log_b(x)}{\log_b(a)}$$

5. Slope-Intercept Equation

The standard equation of a straight line in Cartesian coordinates: $$y = mx + b$$

---

Calculus Formulas

6. Limit Definition of the Derivative

Represents the instantaneous rate of change of a function: $$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

7. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Connects differentiation and integration: $$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

8. Taylor Series Expansion

Represents a function as an infinite sum of terms calculated from the values of its derivatives at a single point: $$f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!} (x-a)^n$$

9. Integration by Parts

A rule that transforms the integral of a product of functions: $$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$$

10. Euler's Identity

A famous equation in complex analysis relating five fundamental mathematical constants: $$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

## 1. The name

**TINDRA** — pronounced *TIN-druh*.

- **Acronym (it is the product):** **T**ext · **I**nput · **N**arration ·
  **D**ictation · **R**eading · **A**ccess. Type it, dictate it, hear it narrated,
  read it back, and reach it however you work — that is the whole product in six
  letters.
- **Meaning:** *tindra* is Swedish for "to twinkle / shine." Light and clarity —
  a fitting note for a low-vision and blind audience, and a quiet echo of QUILL's
  feather-and-light heritage without sitting in the crowded English "quill / pen /
  voice / aura" cluster everyone else reaches for.
- **Why it survived vetting:** of a dozen candidates checked, TINDRA was the only
  one with **no collision in the accessibility / writing / AI software category**
  (its only same-name uses are in unrelated classes — a furniture brand, a generic
  IT shop). Competitors like *Aura Reader*, *Wren AI*, *Penna*, *Plume*, and
  *Saylo* are all already writing/AI apps. See `docs/` naming research notes.
- **Lineage from QUILL:** position the change as an evolution, not a reboot —
  "QUILL is now TINDRA." Same team (Community Access), same promises, a name we
  can actually own and defend.

> Gate: TINDRA is not pristine, and a **June 2026 finding has weakened it
> materially**: `tindra.app` is a live commercial software product (a Shopify
> analytics tool). That is a **same-broad-class software collision under the
> exact name**, and it takes the previously recommended `tindra.app` domain off
> the table. The `.store` domain is held by a furniture brand. Do not treat
> TINDRA as cleared — see the updated verdict in section 11; a Class 9/42
> trademark search (now with a known competitor to search for) is required
> before any public use, and a name pivot is on the table.

## 2. What TINDRA is (one-liner and elevator pitch)

**One-liner:** *TINDRA is the private, on-device writing studio you can see, hear,
and feel — built screen-reader-first for everyone.*

**Elevator pitch:** TINDRA is a desktop writing and reading studio designed from
the first keystroke for blind, low-vision, and print-disabled people — and a joy
for everyone else. Write and edit in plain text, Markdown, HTML, or code;
transcribe and caption audio, dictate, and run commands by voice — all **offline,
on your own machine**; proof and read braille; export accessible DAISY talking
books; and ask an on-device AI assistant that never needs a cloud account.
Everything is optional, nothing is uploaded, and it launches, edits, and saves
with all of it turned off.

## 3. Positioning

**Category:** Accessible writing and reading studio (desktop). Not "a screen
reader," not "another Markdown editor" — the writing environment that assumes a
screen reader, braille display, and voice are first-class, not afterthoughts.

**The core promise:** *Every word, every way.* See it, hear it, feel it, speak it.

**Four brand pillars** (these are the real differentiators, and the public
"Private · Local · Universal · Multimodal" story):

1. **Private** — your words and audio stay on your machine; no account required;
   AI and network features are opt-in and audited.
2. **Local** — on-device transcription, dictation, and AI (whisper.cpp / Faster
   Whisper / llama.cpp / Apple Foundation Models). Works offline.
3. **Universal** — screen-reader-first, braille-native, low-vision-aware,
   keyboard-complete. Accessibility is the architecture, not a setting.
4. **Multimodal** — one document, read and authored as text, speech, and braille,
   with a verbosity system that lets you tune exactly how much TINDRA says.

## 7. The sell (value props, proof, objections)

**Value stack:**

- *Independence* — do the whole writing-and-reading loop yourself, your way.
- *Privacy* — nothing uploaded; AI and speech are local; opt-in and audited.
- *One tool* — replaces a pile of separate readers, transcribers, and utilities.
- *No subscription tax* — on-device models, not metered cloud APIs.

**Proof points:** open-source MIT speech models with verified, pinned downloads; a
network-egress audit; DPAPI-encrypted secrets; Safe Mode; braille that never
mutates your file; DAISY 2.02 export to real talking-book players.

**Objections and answers:**

- *"Isn't this just a screen reader?"* No — it is the app you write in; your
  screen reader still runs, and TINDRA is built so it never fights it.
- *"Do I need the internet / an account?"* No. It runs fully offline; the only
  network actions are explicit, consented downloads (models, optional ffmpeg).
- *"Is the AI sending my work to the cloud?"* No — Ask TINDRA runs on-device by
  default; cloud is strictly opt-in.
- *"Will it work with my setup?"* Windows primary, macOS supported; works with
  JAWS, NVDA, Narrator, and VoiceOver.

## 10. Rename / migration plan (engineering blast radius)

A rename ripples widely. Sequence it so nothing breaks for existing users.

**Inventory of what carries the name:**

- Product strings: title bar, About, menus, dialogs, status bar, wizard.
- Identifiers: `quill` Python package/module, `python -m quill`, settings dir
  (`%APPDATA%\Quill`), installer `AppId`/`AppName`/`OutputBaseFilename`, the
  `quill.exe` launcher, file associations, "Send to Quill" shell verbs,
  `build/version.toml` product_name.
- Extensions: `Quillins`, `quillins_bundled/`, the manifest schema, `@quill/api`.
- Brand assets: README, user guide, PRD, release notes, website, GitHub org/repo
  (`Community-Access/quill`), update feed/appcast.
- Credentials/keys: `QUILL:huggingface:token`, DPAPI key labels.

**Migration principles:**

1. **Keep the data dir and on-disk identity stable across the rename**, or migrate
   it explicitly with a one-time, announced move (mirror the existing data-location
   move logic) — never silently orphan a user's settings, dictionaries, autosaves.
2. **Honor old shortcuts/associations** during a transition window (the installer
   already removes stale shortcuts on upgrade — reuse that path).
3. **Two-phase strings:** display name first (TINDRA in the UI), internal
   identifiers (package name, AppId) second, behind a release boundary, since
   AppId/package changes affect upgrade-in-place.
4. **`Quillins` → `Sparks`** is the largest code/doc rename; keep a compatibility
   alias for existing manifests for one release.

**Suggested order:** (a) clear the name legally → (b) display strings + brand docs
→ (c) website/org/domain + update feed → (d) extension rebrand (Sparks) with alias
→ (e) deep identifier rename (package, AppId, data dir migration) in a major
version with a guided upgrade.

## 11. Trademark and domain clearance — the gate (DO THIS FIRST)

### Do we even need this for an open-source project? (Yes — but cheaply.)

A free/MIT project does **not** get a pass on trademark. Why:

- Trademark turns on **"use in commerce"** (broadly read — distributing free
  software with a website, downloads, and app-store listings counts) and
  **likelihood of confusion**. You do not have to charge money to be using a name
  in a way trademark law cares about.
- The risk for a free project is not owing damages — it is being **forced to
  rename** by a prior holder's cease-and-desist. An injunction (a "stop using it"
  order) does not require that you profited.
- **Open-source licenses cover copyright, not the name.** MIT/GPL explicitly tend
  to *exclude* trademarks. (Debian shipped Firefox as **"Iceweasel"** for years
  purely over Mozilla's trademark, even though the code was free.)
- App stores and domain registrars have their own takedown/dispute processes that
  do not care whether you profit.

**What being free / open-source genuinely changes:** you can skip *registering your
own* mark (registration is optional), and you can clear the name for **$0** with a
DIY knockout. **What it does not change:** you should still run that free knockout
before building brand and community — which is exactly what this project is doing
(app stores, a website, marketing to the blind community, consolidating other
products under the name). The expensive part is optional; the cheap check is not.

(This is general principle, not legal advice.)

Web research narrows the field; it does **not** clear a name. Before any public
use of TINDRA:

1. **USPTO Class 9 search** (computer software) + the specific goods
   (word-processing / accessibility software), ideally via a trademark attorney —
   plus international classes if you sell outside the US.
2. **Domain — the exact-match names are already taken.** Verified via WHOIS:
   `tindra.com` is registered (since **2006**, IONOS, transfer-locked) and
   `tindra.org` is registered (since **2015**, Key-Systems, owner redacted);
   `tindra.store` is the furniture brand and the known "Tindra" brands also hold
   `tindra-design.com` and `tindrah.com`. "Tindra" is also a common Swedish given
   name, so exact-match domains across TLDs are largely claimed.
   - **`tindra.app` is now taken** by a live commercial software product (see
     the June 2026 update at the top of this section), so it is **no longer an
     option** despite being the earlier recommendation.
   - **Options:** (a) pursue a **broker purchase** of `tindra.com`/`.org` if the
     budget and a willing seller exist (premium, uncertain) — confirm with an
     authoritative registrar WHOIS first; or (b) **go prefixed/alternate** and
     own it cleanly: `usetindra.com`, `gettindra.com` / `.org`, `tindra.io`, or
     `tindra.studio`. For a community/nonprofit publisher (Community Access), a
     prefixed `.org` (e.g., `gettindra.org`) reads well and is far cheaper than
     chasing the 2006-era `.com` — **but** a prefixed domain does not cure the
     same-class software collision that the `tindra.app` product creates;
     resolve the trademark question (or pivot) first.
   - A free domain is **not** the same as a clear trademark — keep step 1 (Class 9
     search) as the real gate; lock matching socials and the GitHub org alongside.
3. **App stores / package registries:** check the Microsoft Store, Mac App Store,
   npm/PyPI for `tindra` collisions.
4. **Defensive filing:** once cleared, file the mark in the relevant classes before
   launch; secure `Sparks` (or the chosen extension name) too if distinctive.

### What this costs (indicative, 2026, US — not legal or financial advice)

Ballpark ranges. Actual figures vary by attorney, firm, jurisdiction, and how many
trademark classes you file in. Treat these as planning numbers, not quotes.

**Trademark search and clearance**

| Step | Typical cost (USD) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| DIY USPTO search (free TESS-style search) | $0 | Cheap insurance, but **not** reliable on its own — misses similar/common-law marks |
| Attorney "knockout" / preliminary search + read | $300 – $800 | Fast same-week go/no-go; the cost-effective first real step |
| Full comprehensive clearance search (search-firm report, e.g. Corsearch/CompuMark) | $700 – $2,500 | Federal + state + common-law + domains |
| Attorney written clearance opinion | $1,000 – $2,500 | Recommended before spending on rebrand/launch |
| **Realistic all-in clearance** | **$1,500 – $5,000** | Knockout + comprehensive + opinion |

**Filing the trademark (after it clears)**

| Item | Typical cost (USD) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| USPTO government fee | ~$350 per class | Class 9 (software) at minimum; possibly Class 42 (SaaS) |
| Attorney filing fee | $500 – $1,500 per class | Some firms bundle search + file |
| **Per-class total to file** | **~$850 – $2,000** | Budget 1–2 classes |
| International (Madrid Protocol), optional | $3,000 – $10,000+ | Only if selling outside the US under the mark |

**Domains**

| Item | Typical cost (USD) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Alternate TLDs (`usetindra.com`, `gettindra.org`, `tindra.io`, `tindra.studio`) | $10 – $60 / year each | The cheap route — but `tindra.app` is taken (analytics product), and a prefixed domain does not cure the same-class trademark question |
| Broker purchase of `tindra.org` | ~$500 – $5,000 | Uncertain; depends on the owner |
| Broker purchase of `tindra.com` (6-letter, held since 2006) | ~$2,000 – $30,000+ | Premium and uncertain; the owner may simply not sell |

**Bottom line.** A sane, defensible budget is roughly **$2,000 – $6,000** all-in:
a knockout-plus-comprehensive clearance with an attorney opinion (~$1,500–$5,000),
one or two class filings (~$850–$2,000 each), and cheap alternate domains
($10–$60/yr). Add a premium domain acquisition only if you specifically want the
exact-match `.com`/`.org` and have the budget for it. Start with the **$300–$800
knockout search** — it is the cheapest way to learn early whether TINDRA is worth
spending the rest on.

### Suggested vendors (starting points, not an endorsement)

Verify current fees, reviews, and that you are working with a **licensed U.S.
trademark attorney** before engaging. Match the vendor to the goal: for a real
go/no-go and a defensible launch you want a *comprehensive search plus an attorney
opinion*, not just a filing service.

**1. Do this yourself first (free preliminary "knockout"):**

- **USPTO trademark search** — `search.uspto.gov` (the official tool that replaced
  TESS). Search the exact mark and close variants in Class 9 / 42.
- **Trademarkia** — free public search for a quick read of existing marks.
- **Domains / handles** — a registrar (Namecheap, Cloudflare, GoDaddy) for an
  available alternate (`usetindra.com`, `gettindra.org`, etc. — `tindra.app` is
  taken); Namechk or similar for social handles.

**2. Flat-fee trademark attorneys (the recommended path — search + opinion + file):**

- **Gerben IP** (gerbenlaw.com) — well-established; flat ~**$3,000** covers a
  comprehensive federal/state/common-law search, an attorney **opinion letter**,
  and filing in up to two classes (plus USPTO fees). Good all-in option.
- **Dawsey IP** — ~**$850** flat for a comprehensive U.S. search; single-class
  filing free of attorney fee (you still pay the ~$350/class USPTO fee). Strong
  value for the search step.
- **Flat Fee Trademark** (flatfeetrademark.com), **Knowmad Law**, **The Trademark
  Firm**, **Counsel for Creators**, **Cognition IP** (startup-focused) — other
  reputable flat-fee attorney options worth quoting.

**3. Budget DIY-with-filing (cheapest, but shallowest — use with caution):**

- **Trademark Engine**, **LegalZoom**, **Trademarkia (filing service)**, **Bizee**,
  **Rocket Lawyer** — these *file* inexpensively, but their "search" is typically
  shallow and they do not give a real clearance opinion. Fine for a low-stakes
  mark; **not** a substitute for an attorney clearance before a rebrand investment.

**4. Comprehensive search-report firms (usually accessed *through* your attorney):**

- **Corsearch** and **CompuMark (Clarivate)** — the professional full-search
  reports an attorney orders and interprets. You rarely buy these directly.

**5. Finding / vetting an attorney:**

- **INTA** (International Trademark Association) member directory; your **state bar**
  referral service; reviews on **Avvo** / **Martindale**.

**Recommended sequence:** start with a **~$300–$850 attorney knockout or
comprehensive search** (e.g., Dawsey's ~$850 comprehensive, or a knockout from a
flat-fee firm) → if it comes back clean, proceed to the **opinion + filing**
(Gerben's bundled ~$3,000, or search + per-class filing elsewhere) → register
alternate domains in parallel. Spend nothing on rebrand or premium domains until
the knockout passes.

If clearance fails, the runner-up process is the same: pick the next distinctive
coinage and re-run steps 1–4. Do not skip this for speed.

- **Name still blocked at clearance.** Mitigation: keep 1–2 vetted alternates

- **Existing QUILL users confused by the change.** Mitigation: "QUILL is now
  TINDRA" everywhere for a release; in-app one-time notice; unchanged data and
  workflows.
- **Upgrade-in-place breakage from AppId/package rename.** Mitigation: stage it

- **SEO reset / loss of QUILL search equity.** Mitigation: 301s from old pages,
  keep QUILL as a documented former name, lean on the acronym and feature terms.
- **"Twinkle" reads as frivolous to enterprise buyers.** Mitigation: lead with the
  acronym and the Private/Local/Universal/Multimodal pillars in institutional
  materials; save the poetry for consumer copy.

## 15. Extensions naming: TINDRA Sparks

**"Quillins" should probably retire with QUILL rather than being awkwardly
reshaped into "Tindrins."** The best replacement is **TINDRA Sparks**.

An individual plugin is a **Spark**. Collectively, they are **TINDRA Sparks**.

It fits the TINDRA identity (twinkle / shine) and gives an entire vocabulary:

- **Extensions** — the plain-language technical term
- **TINDRA Sparks** — the friendly branded name
- **Spark Gallery** — where users discover extensions
- **Spark Manager** — where users install, update, enable, and remove them
- **Spark SDK** — developer tooling
- **Spark Pack** — a curated bundle
- **Create a Spark** — developer documentation
- **Featured Sparks** — recommended extensions

The interface can preserve clarity by combining both terms:

> **Extensions: TINDRA Sparks**
> Add new abilities to TINDRA with extensions called Sparks.

That dual framing matters for accessibility and localization. A new user
immediately understands what the feature does, while the brand still feels
magical.

### A complete naming family

Use different names for different types of enhancement instead of calling
everything a plugin:

| Capability | Branded name | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General plugin | **Spark** | Markdown Tools Spark |
| AI assistant or agent | **Companion** | Editorial Companion |
| Visual appearance | **Theme** | Midnight High Contrast Theme |
| Language support | **Language Pack** | Spanish Language Pack |
| Collection of extensions | **Spark Pack** | Accessible Authoring Spark Pack |
| Reusable automation | **Workflow** | Publish to WordPress Workflow |
| Snippet collection | **Snippet Pack** | Business Writing Snippet Pack |

This keeps the ecosystem coherent without flattening everything to "plugin."

### Package and repository conventions

Establish predictable technical names:

```text
tindra-spark-markdown
tindra-spark-github
tindra-spark-pandoc
tindra-companion-editorial
tindra-companion-accessibility
tindra-theme-high-contrast
tindra-language-pack-es
tindra-spark-pack-publishing
```

Python package namespaces follow:

```python
tindra_sparks.markdown
tindra_sparks.github
tindra_companions.editorial
```

A manifest might be:

```json
{
  "id": "community-access.markdown-tools",
  "name": "Markdown Tools",
  "type": "spark",
  "displayType": "Extension",
  "publisher": "Community Access",
  "tindraVersion": ">=1.0"
}
```

Do **not** require "Spark" in the user-facing extension name. The Gallery can
display:

> **Markdown Tools**
> Spark by Community Access

That prevents repetitive names such as "Markdown Tools Spark Spark."

### What to do with Quillins

Treat **Quillin** as a legacy ecosystem term during migration:

> Quillins are now called TINDRA Sparks.

For one or two releases:

- Continue recognizing old Quillin manifests and package identifiers.
- Display them as **Legacy Quillin extensions** only where necessary.
- Provide an automated manifest migration.
- Redirect old documentation and repository links.
- Avoid forcing developers to rename package IDs immediately.
- Reserve "Quillin" permanently as a compatibility term rather than reusing it
  elsewhere.

A friendly release-note line:

> The Quillins have found their spark. Plugins are now known as TINDRA Sparks.

### Other good possibilities

- **TINDRA Glimmers** is charming, but "Glimmer Manager" and "Glimmer SDK" are
  less immediately understandable.
- **TINDRA Embers** has energy, but an ember can suggest something fading rather
  than something adding capability.
- **TINDRA Runes** feels magical, but introduces pronunciation, spelling, and
  fantasy associations that may not suit everyone.
- **TINDRA Add-ons** is extremely clear but loses the distinctive identity.
- **TINDRA Modules** is technically safe but not especially delightful.

**Strongest recommendation:**

> **Official generic term:** Extension
> **TINDRA-branded term:** Spark
> **Collective ecosystem:** TINDRA Sparks
> **Discovery experience:** Spark Gallery
> **Agentic extensions:** TINDRA Companions

That gives TINDRA a distinctive vocabulary without making users learn jargon
before they can understand the product.

## 16. Renaming and rebranding mechanics — file-naming conventions and identifiers

This is the concrete companion to section 10. Section 10 sequences the
engineering blast radius; this section pins the **actual file, package, and
identifier conventions** to change and what "done" looks like. Two independent
renames are in scope and **must be staged separately** so upgrade-in-place never
breaks:

- **Product:** QUILL → **TINDRA** (display strings first, identifiers later)
- **Extensions:** Quillin → **Spark** (see section 15; ship with an alias)

### Identifier and path mapping — product (QUILL → TINDRA)

| Kind | Today | TINDRA | Migration note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Python package | `quill/` (and `quill.core`, `quill.io`, `quill.ui`, `quill.platform`, `quill.stability`, `quill.tools`, `quill.plugins`) | `tindra/` (same submodules) | Deep change; do in a major version. Keep a `quill` shim that re-exports `tindra` for one release if anything external imports it |
| Module entry point | `python -m quill` | `python -m tindra` | Document both for a transition window |
| Launcher / exe | `quill.exe` | `tindra.exe` | Installer must remove the old shortcut and register the new target |
| Installer identity | `installer/quill.iss` — `AppId`, `AppName`, `OutputBaseFilename=Quill-for-All-Setup-<v>` | `tindra.iss` — new `AppName`/`OutputBaseFilename`; **`AppId` change is breaking** | Generate via the iss generator (`build_inno_setup_script`), not by hand. A new `AppId` means a side-by-side install, not an upgrade — plan a guided data migration if changed |
| Data directory | `%APPDATA%\Quill` | `%APPDATA%\TINDRA` | Either keep the `Quill` path stable across the rename, or do a one-time announced move mirroring the existing data-location move logic — never silently orphan settings, dictionaries, autosaves, backups |
| Shell verbs | "Send to Quill" / core verb registry | "Send to TINDRA" | Re-register; the verb registry feeds `build_inno_setup_script`, so regenerate the iss |
| Build identity | `build/version.toml` `product_name = "QUILL for All"`, `APP_DISPLAY_NAME` | `product_name = "TINDRA"` | `build/version.toml` is the single human-edited source; everything else generates from it |
| Credential / key labels | `QUILL:huggingface:token`, DPAPI key labels | `TINDRA:...` | Migrate stored secrets on first run or re-prompt; do not strand existing tokens |
| Update feed / appcast | QUILL appcast URL | TINDRA appcast | 301 the old feed; old clients must still resolve to an upgrade path |
| GitHub org/repo | `Community-Access/quill` | repo rename or new repo | GitHub redirects old URLs automatically on rename; keep the redirect |
| Node API package | `@quill/api` | `@tindra/api` | Publish the new scope; keep `@quill/api` as a deprecated re-export for one release |

### Identifier and path mapping — extensions (Quillin → Spark)

| Kind | Today | Spark | Migration note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bundled directory | `quill/quillins_bundled/` | `tindra/sparks_bundled/` | Move with history; keep loader able to read both during transition |
| Manifest type field | `"type": "quillin"` (`quill/core/schemas/extension.json`) | `"type": "spark"` (+ `companion`, `theme`, `language-pack`, `spark-pack`, `workflow`, `snippet-pack`) | Schema accepts both old and new `type` for one release; auto-migrate on load |
| Lint tool | `quill/tools/quillin_lint.py` (`python -m quill.tools.quillin_lint`) | `spark_lint` (`python -m tindra.tools.spark_lint`) | Alias the old console command |
| Node runner | `node_quillin_runner` + `word-count-node` example | `node_spark_runner` + renamed example | Keep old runner name resolvable for legacy manifests |
| Distribution ids | (n/a — "Quillin" naming) | `tindra-spark-<name>`, `tindra-companion-<name>`, `tindra-theme-<name>`, `tindra-language-pack-<code>`, `tindra-spark-pack-<name>` | New, predictable convention from section 15 |
| Python namespaces | (n/a) | `tindra_sparks.<name>`, `tindra_companions.<name>` | New convention |

### Naming conventions to adopt

- **Repos / packages:** lowercase, hyphenated, type-prefixed —
  `tindra-spark-*`, `tindra-companion-*`, `tindra-theme-*`,
  `tindra-language-pack-<code>`, `tindra-spark-pack-*`.
- **Python imports:** lowercase, underscored namespace packages —
  `tindra_sparks.*`, `tindra_companions.*`.
- **Manifest:** `id` is `publisher.short-name` (e.g.
  `community-access.markdown-tools`); `type` is the machine category; optional
  `displayType` is the human label shown in the Gallery; `tindraVersion` is a
  semver range gating compatibility.
- **User-facing names:** never embed "Spark" in the extension's own `name`; the
  Gallery composes "Spark by <publisher>" so names don't stutter.

### Sequencing and back-compat

1. **Phase 1 (surface):** display strings only — title bar, About, menus,
   wizard, Gallery copy show **TINDRA** and **Sparks**. No identifier changes;
   fully reversible.
2. **Phase 2 (Sparks ecosystem):** rename `quillins_bundled` → `sparks_bundled`,
   add the new `type` values and namespaces, ship the manifest migrator, keep
   the `quillin` alias and legacy-manifest loader. Old extensions still load.
3. **Phase 3 (deep identifiers, major version):** `quill` → `tindra` package,
   `AppId`, data-dir migration, `quill.exe` → `tindra.exe`, credential labels,
   `@quill/api` → `@tindra/api` — all guided, announced, and reversible.

### Definition of done / CI gates to update

- **GATE-VC (version consistency):** teach it the new `product_name` and any
  renamed installer fields; it must still agree across `build/version.toml`,
  `__init__.py`, the iss, and CHANGELOG.
- **iss generator + sync test:** regenerate `tindra.iss` from
  `build_inno_setup_script`; the committed-iss-in-sync test must pass for the new
  identity.
- **Spark lint / manifest schema tests:** accept both `quillin` and `spark`
  manifests until the alias window closes, then drop `quillin`.
- **Module/import-boundary checks:** update any `quill`-prefixed path
  assertions to `tindra`; keep a temporary `quill` re-export shim covered by a
  deprecation test.
- **Docs parity:** README, user guide, PRD, release notes, and the Spark SDK
  docs all use TINDRA / Spark vocabulary; old doc URLs 301 to the new ones.

> Reminder: `build/version.toml` is the single human-edited source of truth for
> release identity, and it is local-only (gitignored). Drive every generated
> name (installer, build-info module, display strings) from it — do not hard-code
> the product or channel name in committed code.

## 17. Alternate names (fallback if TINDRA is blocked)

Because the `tindra.app` same-class collision puts a TINDRA launch on uphill
footing (section 11), keep a vetted shortlist ready. These are **acronym/
backronym** candidates whose letters genuinely map to the product, chosen to be
pronounceable and easy to spell with a screen reader, and leaning on the same
light/clarity heritage. **None are cleared** — each still needs the section 11
USPTO Class 9/42 + domain check before use. Collision flags below are
preliminary, not a clearance.

### Top tier

| Name | Backronym | Theme / why | Collision risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **AVRA** | **A**ccessible · **V**oice · **R**eading · **A**uthoring | Coined, short, distinctive, trivially spellable (AV-rah) | Low-ish — other uses are a Greek given name / restaurant (unrelated class); verify software |
| **VERBA** | **V**oice · **E**diting · **R**eading · **B**raille · **A**ccess | Latin for "words" — literally on-point for a writing studio (VER-bah) | Low-medium; verify |
| **LUMA** | **L**ocal · **U**niversal · **M**ultimodal · **A**ccess | Maps to three brand pillars; luminance/"shine" carries the TINDRA light motif (LOO-mah) | Higher — Luma AI (video), luma.com (events); verify hard |

### Runners-up

| Name | Backronym | Theme / why | Collision risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **SONARA** | **S**peech · **O**ffline · **N**arration · **A**uthoring · **R**eading · **A**ccess | Sound/listening theme; all six letters true | Medium — a "Sonara AI" may exist; check |
| **VELA** | **V**oice · **E**diting · **L**ocal · **A**ccess | Constellation ("the sails") — light/navigation | Medium — pronunciation ambiguity (VEE-la/VAY-la); some game/brand uses |
| **CLARA** | **C**lear · **L**ocal · **A**uthoring · **R**eading · **A**ccess | "Clarity" is squarely on-brand for low vision | Medium-high — common given name + Clara.io / Clara AI |

### Resonant but avoid

- **ARIA** — reads as **A**ccessible **R**eading, **I**nput & **A**uthoring, and
  WAI-**ARIA** *is* the accessibility standard, so the resonance is perfect — but
  that is exactly why it is not ownable and is confusing. Great metaphor, bad
  trademark.
- **PRISM / PRISMA** — a prism splits one light into many (one document → text,
  speech, braille) is a lovely metaphor, but collisions are heavy (Prisma ORM,
  Adobe) and "Prism" is already used internally for the screen-reader bridge.
  Keep as copy, not a name.
- **NOVA / AURA / AVID / SAGE / VISTA** — all spell nicely and fit, but each is an
  established software / AI / media product (or Windows Vista). Distinctiveness
  too low.

**Pick order for distinctiveness + clean field + on-meaning:** AVRA, then VERBA,
then LUMA (if it clears). AVRA and VERBA are likeliest to survive a Class 9
search because they are coined or Latin rather than a trendy English tech term.
